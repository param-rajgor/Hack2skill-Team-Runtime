## Red Flags

The JD explicitly or implicitly discourages:

### Career Red Flags
- Entire career in service-based consulting companies
- No product-company exposure
- No ownership of shipped systems

Examples:
- TCS-only career
- Infosys-only career
- Wipro-only career
- Cognizant-only career
- Capgemini-only career

### Technical Red Flags
- Only theoretical AI knowledge
- No production deployments
- Only course/project experience
- No evaluation experience
- No retrieval/ranking exposure

### AI Red Flags
- Recently entered AI because of ChatGPT hype
- LangChain-only experience
- Prompt-engineering-only experience
- No evidence of actual ML systems

### Domain Red Flags
- Pure Computer Vision background
- Pure Research background
- Academic-only profile

## What Recruiter Actually Wants

The JD is not looking for the candidate with the most AI buzzwords.

The recruiter wants someone who:

1. Has actually built and shipped production AI systems.
2. Understands retrieval, ranking, search, and recommendation systems.
3. Knows how to evaluate models using metrics and experimentation.
4. Can write production-quality Python code.
5. Has worked in product-focused environments.
6. Can move fast and work with ambiguity.
7. Understands both engineering and business impact.
8. Has genuine AI experience rather than recently acquired buzzword knowledge.

### Ideal Candidate Summary

A strong candidate would look like:

- 6–8 years experience
- Strong Python background
- Production ML experience
- Retrieval/Ranking/Search experience
- Evaluation experience (NDCG, MRR, MAP, A/B Testing)
- Product-company background
- Evidence of shipped systems
- High ownership and execution ability
